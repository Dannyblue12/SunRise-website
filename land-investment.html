Why Trust Us?

SunRise Cooperative Society Ltd—the only FMBN-certified Cooperative in Akwa Ibom State—is leading the mission to help workers and income earners own affordable homes through the National Housing Scheme (NHS).

This programme is backed by the Nigerian Constitution (Act No. 2 of 1992 and Act No. 3 of 1992), which guarantees every Nigerian’s right to home ownership.

We are committed to helping workers, artisans, farmers, and business owners access mortgage funding, secure their housing documents, and transition into genuine homeowners.

Why You Must Join a Cooperative

To access the housing scheme, applicants must belong to a cooperative.

Affordable For Everyone

Understanding the Financial Requirements & House Qualification

Monthly NHF Contribution

Contribution Requirement
₦1,750 /month
(2.5% of National Minimum Wage Basic Salary ₦70,000)

Members become eligible after six months of consistent contribution.

For business owners and farmers, contributions are done monthly through the cooperative.

Affordability Index Method

The Federal Mortgage Bank uses this computation to place everyone in a category they can afford. No one is rejected.

Analysis Basis 33.3% of Monthly Income
Proof Required Last 12-Month Bank Statement
Factors Business/Salary Inflow

Documentation & Location Support

What If You Have No Land Documents?
SunRise Cooperative assists in:
  • Securing State-issued Letter of Consent
  • Processing Right of Occupancy (R of O) from your Local Government
  • Facilitating C of O eventually

These documents guarantee you are fully protected and legally approved to build.

For Applicants Outside Uyo or in LGAs

Your Local Government can issue: Right of Occupancy (R of O)

While the State Government issues: Letter of Consent (LOP)

SunRise Cooperative coordinates this process on your behalf.

Example: Land Sharing for Affordable Home Construction

A standard 1,000 sqm plot can be shared among 4 applicants:

  • Each person gets 250 sqm
  • Enough for a full self-contained structure
  • Cost becomes more affordable
  • Cooperative finances and structures the development

This method helps low and middle-income earners own property easily.
